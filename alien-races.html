Topic 07

Alien Races

Descriptions of nonhuman visitors vary wildly, yet a few recurring forms dominate the literature. The question is never just what they look like, but what their repeated appearance says about fear, memory, or contact.

Review the typologies Back to home
Classification problem

Witnesses report categories as if they are known already, which gives the lore a strange feeling of continuity.

Recurring forms

  • Small gray figures dominate abduction narratives and laboratory-like environments.
  • Taller humanoid visitors tend to appear in contact stories with moral or civilizational messages.

Motives assigned

  • Accounts describe observation, warning, genetic curiosity, or intervention.
  • These motives often reveal as much about human anxiety as alien intent.

Contact and symbol

  • Some researchers treat alien races as literal visitors, others as psychic masks for deeper phenomena.
  • The category refuses to settle into one explanation.
